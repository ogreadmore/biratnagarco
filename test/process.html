Dispatch Process Guide

Phone‑first steps for each service. Every process starts with an inbound call or social message, then confirm details, secure the resource, collect payment, and deliver with clear updates.

Overview: How this business works

You are a coordinator. You don’t need an office or a computer. With only your phone, you answer calls and messages, understand what the customer needs, then match them with a trusted driver, guard, technician, or vehicle. You earn a clear coordination fee (your margin) for making everything happen smoothly.

The simple idea

  • Customer contacts you by call/WhatsApp/Facebook.
  • You collect details (what, where, when, budget, special needs).
  • You check your trusted provider list for availability and price.
  • You send the customer a clear quote and get a confirmation (and deposit if needed).
  • You lock the provider, share their name/number/ETA, and keep both sides updated.
  • After the job, you collect payment, pay the provider, and keep your margin.

A day‑in‑the‑life (story)

  1. Inquiry arrives: A shop owner messages, “Need a small truck today 4pm, Main Road → Rani.”
  2. Clarify details: You ask for pickup address, drop address, load size, help needed, and budget.
  3. Check providers: You ping 2–3 drivers. One confirms: small pickup, NPR 3,500 at 4pm.
  4. Quote and confirm: You send the customer a WhatsApp quote (what’s included, any waiting fees) and get OK + small deposit.
  5. Book and share info: You confirm the driver and send driver name, phone, vehicle, ETA to the customer.
  6. Updates and finish: You share “arrived • loaded • delivered”, then collect the balance and message a receipt.

Words you’ll hear (plain meaning)

  • Client/Customer: the person or company hiring you.
  • Provider: the driver, guard, technician, or vendor who does the job.
  • Quote: the price you send the client with what’s included/excluded.
  • Deposit/Advance: part payment before the job to lock the booking.
  • Margin/Fee: your earnings for coordination (transparent and fair).
  • Receipt/Invoice: a simple message stating work done and amount paid/owed.
  • ETA: estimated time of arrival.

Money basics (phone‑only)

  • Accept: mobile wallet, bank transfer, or cash. Share your wallet number or QR.
  • For bigger jobs, take a deposit to avoid last‑minute cancellations.
  • After completion, collect the balance, pay the provider promptly, keep your margin.
  • Keep a simple note of each job: date, client, provider, price, your margin.

Your job is communication and reliability. Be polite, be clear, and always share updates. If there’s a delay or change, inform both the customer and the provider early.

Local Freight Dispatch

Book the right vehicle and driver for pickup and delivery within city or corridor. Keep updates simple on WhatsApp.

  1. Inbound call/message Ask: pickup & drop locations, ready time, load type/weight/size, offloading help, budget.
  2. Price & availability Ping 2–3 trusted drivers: share route, load, time; confirm vehicle match and rate.
  3. Quote & confirm Send WhatsApp quote with total, what’s included, any waiting/extra fees, and deposit if needed.
  4. Secure booking Collect deposit (mobile wallet/bank) and send driver name/number, vehicle, ETA in one message.
  5. Live updates Share: en‑route, arrived pickup, loaded, departed, delivered. Ask for photo at delivery if useful.
  6. Close & payment Send final amount; collect remaining via wallet/cash; issue WhatsApp receipt text.

Ready‑to‑use messages

  • Quote: Route [Pickup → Drop], Time [Time], Vehicle [Type], Load [Weight/Notes], Price NPR [Amount] (fuel/driver incl.). Extras: waiting >30min, extra stops billed. Deposit NPR [Amount/0].
  • Confirm: Driver [Name, Number], Vehicle [Type/Plate], ETA [Time]. Notes: gate pass/contact at pickup?

Vehicles & Airport Taxi

Match rider to clean vehicle and polite driver. Be on time and clear on price.

  1. Inbound details Name, date/time, pickup, drop, passengers, luggage count, child seat, AC, budget.
  2. Assign driver Check availability in driver list; confirm price and vehicle class (sedan/SUV/van).
  3. Send quote Fixed price with inclusions (airport parking, tolls) and waiting rules.
  4. Confirm & deposit Collect deposit if peak time/long trip; share driver profile and ETA.
  5. Day‑of Remind driver 30–60 min before; share live pin or signboard text for airport.
  6. Payment & receipt Wallet/cash after drop; message a receipt with trip summary.

Quick messages

  • “Hi [Name], your driver [Name], [Phone], [Vehicle] will arrive at [Time].”
  • “Airport pickup: driver will hold a sign ‘[TEXT]’ at arrivals.”
  • “Waiting charge starts after 30 mins at NPR [Rate]/hr.”

Repairs Dispatch

Send the right technician with clear scope and arrival window.

  1. Inbound issue Ask issue, address, access notes, photos/video on WhatsApp, urgency.
  2. Scope & estimate Share call‑out fee and labor range; confirm parts policy (actuals + margin).
  3. Assign pro Pick vetted tech by trade; confirm availability and arrival window.
  4. Confirm visit Send tech name/number and window; collect call‑out prepayment if needed.
  5. On site Tech sends short update + photo before/after; get client OK for extra costs.
  6. Close & pay Share final bill; collect wallet/cash; send receipt and 7‑day workmanship note if offered.

Call‑out policy

  • Call‑out fee: NPR [Amount] (includes first 30 mins)
  • Labor after: NPR [Rate]/hr in 30‑min blocks
  • Parts: at cost + coordination margin

Private Security

Place reliable, briefed guards with correct shift timing and site rules.

  1. Inbound request Location, dates, shift hours, number of guards, duties, uniform, pay range.
  2. Source guards Ping vetted guards; check IDs and recent work; confirm availability and rate.
  3. Quote & SOP Send quote and short site SOP (reporting time, checklist, contact person).
  4. Confirm & advance Collect advance for first shift; share guard names, numbers, and reporting time.
  5. Shift monitoring Guards WhatsApp “arrived/on duty/off duty” with photo if required.
  6. Billing Weekly or event‑end invoice; collect; pay guards promptly.

Guard brief (send)

  • Site [Address], Reporting [Time]
  • Contact [Name/Phone]
  • Duties: entry log, bag check, patrol, incident report
  • Dress [Uniform], phone charged, ID card

Event Logistics

Combine staff, vehicles, and vendors into one clear plan and timeline.

  1. Inbound brief Date/time, venue(s), guest count, services needed (staff/decor/transport), budget.
  2. Plan & price Build mini timeline; collect vendor quotes; add margin; send package price.
  3. Confirm & deposit Book vendors after deposit; share master timeline and contact sheet.
  4. Run day WhatsApp updates at key milestones (setup, arrivals, show start, teardown).
  5. Close & settle Send summary; collect remaining; pay vendors; archive photos/notes.

One‑page plan (checklist)

  • Timeline: setup → event → teardown
  • Contacts: client, venue, lead, vendors, drivers
  • Logistics: load‑in points, parking, permits
  • Payments: deposit date, balance date

Tours & Guides

Offer safe, friendly local tours with clear itinerary and pricing.

  1. Inbound request Travel date, group size, interests (tea/nature/culture), hotel pickup, language.
  2. Itinerary & price Draft simple plan with timings and stops; price vehicle + guide + entry fees.
  3. Confirm & deposit Collect deposit; share final itinerary PDF/photo and guide contact.
  4. Day‑of coordination Guide sends quick updates/photos at key stops; handle changes politely.
  5. Close & reviews Collect balance; ask for a 1‑line review and permission to share.

Tour message pack

  • “Here’s your itinerary image for easy reference.”
  • “Guide: [Name, Phone]. Vehicle: [Type/Plate]. Pickup: [Time].”
  • “Please wear seatbelts and keep valuables secure.”

Universal Phone‑Only Playbook

  1. Intake checklist — Who (name/number), What (service details), Where/When (location/time), Budget (target/range), Extras (photos/videos help).
  2. Quote & confirm — Write total and inclusions; note extras (waiting/extra stops); ask deposit if needed; share provider contact + ETA.
  3. Payments — Accept wallet/bank/cash; share payment QR/number; send WhatsApp receipt text; track payouts to providers.
  4. Quality & follow‑up — Short updates during job; ask for 1‑line feedback; save before/after photos; note learnings for next time.
